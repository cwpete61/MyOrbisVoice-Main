How it works

Four steps from signup to first payout.

The whole flow is self-serve. No application form, no interview, no "tier" gating, no volume thresholds before you can earn.

1

Sign up — instant approval

Create a partner account at the portal. You're approved on the spot — no application form, no interview, no waitlist. Bad actors get removed retroactively, but the front door is open to everyone.

2

Get your tracked link

You're handed a unique referral URL the second you sign up. 30-day attribution cookie — if a visitor lands through your link and converts within 30 days, you get the credit. Click and conversion stats are live in your dashboard from day one.

3

Connect your payout method

Once you've earned $50 in approved commissions, you'll be prompted to connect your payout account and complete tax info (W-9 for U.S. partners, W-8BEN for international). Stripe Connect Express handles both — secure, no separate app to install. Payout connection is rolling out — see your dashboard's get-paid checklist for status.

4

Earn on the 1st and 15th

First subscription: 30-day hold to cover refund risk, then payout on the next 1st or 15th (rolled forward to the next business day if it falls on a weekend). Recurring renewals: no hold — they enter the payout cycle immediately.

Payout timeline

First payout: about 45 days. Every month after: same cycle.

The 30-day holdback only applies to a customer's first subscription charge — that's the only window when Stripe lets a refund or chargeback reverse the sale. Every recurring renewal after that pays with no holdback at all.

  • Day 0 — your referral signs up and pays their first invoice. Commission lands in your dashboard as PENDING.
  • Day 30 — first-charge holdback clears. Commission flips to APPROVED.
  • Day 31-45 — next 1st-or-15th rolls around (skipping weekends). Once your APPROVED balance crosses $50, the payout fires.
  • Month 2 onward — every renewal commission is approved instantly with no hold and joins the next payout cycle. Your dashboard shows pending vs. approved running totals in real time.

Payouts go through Stripe Connect Express. Standard Stripe transfer fees and a small platform fee are deducted from each payout — every line item is itemized in your dashboard. The minimum payout threshold is $50; balances under that simply roll into the next cycle.

Common questions

Everything you need to know before signing up.

How does attribution actually work?

When a visitor clicks your /r/{your-code} partner link, a 30-day first-touch attribution cookie is set on their device. If they sign up and pay during that 30-day window — even if they come back through another channel later — the commission is yours. The system records sessionId, landing path, referrer, and a hashed IP per click for fraud protection.

What's the 30-day holdback for, and does it apply to every payment?

The 30-day hold only applies to a customer's first subscription charge — that's the window during which Stripe lets a refund or chargeback reverse the sale. Recurring renewal commissions have no hold — they enter the next payout cycle the moment the renewal posts. So month two onward, your cycle from charge to payout is roughly 0–15 days.

What's the minimum payout?

$50 in approved commissions. Until your approved balance crosses that threshold, the balance just rolls forward to the next 1st-or-15th cycle. Once you cross $50, the payout fires on the next cycle.

Why pay on the 1st and 15th instead of monthly?

Twice-monthly cycles cut your average wait time from referral conversion to bank deposit roughly in half. If the 1st or 15th lands on a Saturday or Sunday, the payout rolls to the next business day. The first payout for a brand-new customer takes about 30–45 days because of the first-charge holdback; every renewal payment after that is on a much shorter cycle.

How does the LTD (lifetime deal) commission work?

The LTD plan is a one-time $497 payment, so the commission is also one-time: $149.10, paid once. There's no recurring LTD commission because there's no recurring LTD subscription.

Do I need a U.S. business or LLC?

No — individuals are welcome. U.S. partners use a W-9 (no business registration required); international partners use a W-8BEN. Tax form intake and Stripe Connect Express payout setup are currently rolling out — until they're live for your account, the dashboard's get-paid checklist will tell you exactly which step is next. You can sign up, share your link, and accumulate commissions in the meantime.

What if my referral upgrades or downgrades?

Your commission adjusts automatically. Basic → Pro takes you from $59.10/mo to $149.10/mo on the next billing cycle. Downgrade goes the other way. Cancel and the commission stops at the next renewal.

Is there a cap on referrals or earnings?

None. Refer 5 customers or refer 500 — you earn 30% recurring on every one of them. Commission rate is the same for every partner; there's currently no tier system or per-partner override.

Can I refer my own business as my own customer?

No — self-referrals aren't eligible. Same goes for referring an employee or anyone with the same billing details. The program is for genuine third-party referrals.

What happens if I stop being active?

Existing commissions keep paying as long as your referrals stay subscribed. The link stays live. You can request that your account be paused or disabled anytime — your historical commissions still pay out as long as your payout method on file remains reachable.

What can a commission status mean?

Five states show up in your dashboard. PENDING: just earned, in the holdback window (first subscription only). APPROVED: cleared and waiting for the next payout cycle. HOLD: bundled into a payout request you've initiated. PAID: deposited. REVERSED: rolled back because the underlying charge was refunded or disputed.

Can I see exactly what gets deducted from each payout?

Yes. Each processed payout in your dashboard itemizes the gross commission, Stripe Connect platform fee, and Stripe transfer fees. Audit-grade receipts you can hand straight to your accountant.
