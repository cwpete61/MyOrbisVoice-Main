The prompt engine

Generic AI sounds generic. MyOrbisVoice sounds like you.

Most "AI receptionist" tools paste your business name into a generic script. MyOrbisVoice uses a five-layer prompt architecture that braids platform safety rules, your business DNA, the channel context, the agent's specialized role, and live session data into every response.

  • Layer 1 — Platform safety rules. Don't lie. Don't promise outcomes. Defer to humans on legal and medical questions.
  • Layer 2 — Your Business DNA. Identity, services, pricing, hours, escalation rules. Versioned. One-click publish.
  • Layer 3 — Channel overlay. Inbound is warm and brisk. Widget is friendly and fast. Outbound stays under thirty seconds.
  • Layer 4 — Agent role. Appointment specialists know booking rules. Sales agents know pricing. Customer service knows your refund policy.
  • Layer 5 — Live session context. Caller history, time of day, last six turns. Compiled at the start of every call.

After the call

Every conversation is recorded, transcribed, summarized.

The second a call ends, the gateway runs three pipelines in parallel: it fetches the recording from Twilio, post-processes the transcript through GPT-4o-mini to clean ASR artifacts, and generates a 2-3 sentence summary. All visible in your dashboard within 60 seconds.

  • Audio playback — every call streamed via signed Bunny CDN URL, gated by tenant auth.
  • Speaker-labeled transcript — turn-by-turn, timestamped, searchable. Stored as JSONB.
  • AI-generated summary — 2-3 sentence outcome digest in Conversation.summaryText.
  • Structured outcome code — BOOKED, QUALIFIED_LEAD, MISSED_CALL, etc. Drives the campaign router.
  • Auto-tagging — outcome → tag → campaign enrollment. Customer is in your follow-up funnel before they put down the phone.

Data & security

Your data lives in your tenant. Period.

MyOrbisVoice was built multi-tenant from day one — not retrofitted. Your prompts, your transcripts, your contacts are isolated at the database row level.

Tenant-isolated by default

Every read and write goes through tenant-scoped middleware. Cross-tenant access is forbidden at the API layer, not just the UI.

Secrets are write-only

Twilio tokens, Stripe keys, Google refresh tokens — all encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM. Never revealed in the UI, even to platform admins.

Append-only audit log

Every sensitive write is logged: prompt publishes, integration reconnects, impersonation events. No DELETE route exists at the API layer.

OAuth, not passwords

Gmail and Calendar connect via Google OAuth. We never see, store, or transmit your Google password. Standard scoped tokens, revocable any time.

Daily DB backups, 30-day retention

Automated PostgreSQL dumps. Regularly tested with full restore-and-verify procedure. We've done the disaster recovery drill so you don't have to.

Internal-only orchestration

Workflow infrastructure (n8n) runs on an internal-only network. Your customers never touch raw automation infrastructure — only the polished UI.

Common questions

What people ask before signing up.

How fast can I really get a live AI agent on my line?

Most customers complete signup, Business DNA configuration, Google + Twilio connection, and number forwarding inside 20 minutes. The slow path — usually because the customer wants to fully fill out a multi-page Business DNA — is about an hour. There is no manual provisioning step on our side.

Do I need to keep my existing phone number?

Yes. Your published business number stays exactly the same. We provision a Twilio number behind the scenes and you forward your existing number to it. Your customers see no change. If you want to port your existing number to Twilio, that's also supported but takes a few days for carrier release.

What does the agent actually know about my business?

Whatever you put in your Business DNA: services, pricing, hours, escalation rules, prohibited language, language preferences, compliance disclaimers. It's a structured document — not a generic prompt blob — so you can edit any field and one-click republish without re-engineering anything.

Can I customize what the agent sounds like?

Yes — choose from seven curated voices (Zephyr, Despina, Aoede, Charon, Fenrir, Puck, Sulafat). Each one has a different gender, tone, and pacing. Switch in one click — no voice-actor contracts, no studio time. Different channels can use different voices if you want.

What if the AI gets something wrong?

Three layers of defense. First, the platform safety prompt (Layer 1) forbids the agent from making up facts or promising outcomes. Second, your Business DNA (Layer 2) defines escalation conditions — "if the caller asks about X, transfer to Y." Third, every call is recorded and transcribed, so you can review and tighten your DNA over time.

What if my caller wants a human?

The agent transfers cleanly. You configure who to transfer to — a specific phone number, a department, or a "let me take a message" fallback for after-hours. The handoff is announced ("transferring you now to Sarah") and uses live Twilio call control, not a hangup-and-callback.

Will the agent know what's on my Google Calendar?

Yes. We pull live freebusy data from your connected calendar at the start of every appointment-booking conversation. The agent will only offer slots that are actually open, and writes the booked event back to your calendar with a Google Calendar invite to the customer.

How are call costs billed?

Voice minutes are bundled in your plan. Basic gets 500/month, Pro 2,000, Premier 5,000, Enterprise 20,000. Overages are billed at $0.18/min. Twilio routing fees are baked in — there's no separate Twilio bill. The AI compute (Gemini) cost is absorbed by us.
