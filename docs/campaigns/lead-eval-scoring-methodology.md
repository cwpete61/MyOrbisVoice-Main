# Lead Capture Evaluation — Methodology & Scoring System

**What this is:** The repeatable process behind the free 15-minute lead evaluation offered in the social campaign. It turns a few test calls into a defensible, consistent **Lead Capture Score (0–100)** and a one-page scorecard the business owner keeps.

**Why it has to be rigorous:** The whole offer rests on the owner trusting the result. If two evaluators would score the same business differently, the scorecard is worthless. Every criterion below is defined so the answer is observable, not a matter of opinion.

**Channel scope:** Phone-focused — the live test call, after-hours behavior, and the callback. (Web form / SMS can be added later as bonus sections; they're out of scope for the 15-minute version.)

---

## The 15-minute process

Run it in this order. Total hands-on time ≈ 12–15 minutes, plus a callback window you check the next day.

| Min | Step | What you do |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 | **Prep** | Pull their public phone number + listed hours. Note current local time vs. their hours (you'll need to know if your call lands in- or after-hours). Set caller ID to a normal mobile number, not a blocked/unknown one. |
| 2–6 | **Call #1 — Business-hours new-customer call** | Call as a realistic prospect (see script). Count rings, note greeting, note whether they capture your info and offer a next step. Hang up politely. Score categories 1–4. |
| 6–9 | **Call #2 — After-hours / second-line test** | If their listed hours are open now, call back and *let it ring through to voicemail* (or call the after-hours path). If they're closed, Call #1 already was after-hours — run a second call to a different staff path if one exists. Score category 5. |
| 9–12 | **Voicemail + leak notes** | If you reached voicemail, leave a short, genuine message with your name + number + a clear request (so a callback is warranted). Start the callback timer. Write down specifics for the report. |
| 12–15 | **Score + draft scorecard** | Enter scores in the spreadsheet. Note the 2–3 most important fixes. |
| Next day | **Callback check** | Did they call you back? How fast? Score category 6 and finalize. |

**Ethics / honesty rule:** You are running a *real* test as a *real* potential customer with a real need you could plausibly have. Don't waste their time with a fake emergency, don't book an appointment you won't keep, and if they ask, you can be upfront that you're evaluating responsiveness. The point is to measure their normal behavior, not to trick them.

### Test-call scripts (pick the one that fits their vertical)

Keep it natural. You're a new customer with a common need.

- **Home services (HVAC/plumbing/electrical):** "Hi — my [AC / water heater] started acting up and I'm trying to find someone who can take a look. Do you handle that, and roughly what would it cost to come out?"
- **Dental/medical:** "Hi, I'm new to the area and looking for a [dentist]. Are you taking new patients, and how soon could I get in?"
- **Salon/beauty:** "Hi — do you have any openings this week for a [cut/color], and what are your prices?"
- **Legal:** "Hi, I had a [car accident / issue] last week and I'm trying to figure out my options. Do you do free consultations?"
- **Generic:** "Hi — I'm looking into [service]. Are you taking new clients, and what's the next step to get started?"

The script's job is to create a natural opening for them to (a) capture your contact info and (b) offer a next step. A good business does both unprompted.

## The scoring system — 100 points, 6 categories

Each category has a small set of mutually exclusive levels, each with a fixed point value. Pick the one that matches what happened. No partial/fudged points — that's what keeps scores consistent between evaluations.

### 1. Speed to Answer — 20 pts *(business-hours call)*
The single biggest predictor of a captured lead. Measured on Call #1 during their stated open hours.

| Level | Points |
|---|---|
| Answered live within ~4 rings (≈20 sec) | 20 |
| Answered live in 5–8 rings | 13 |
| Answered, but long hold / transferred around before a human | 8 |
| Went to voicemail **during posted open hours** | 4 |
| Rang out with no answer and no voicemail, or busy signal | 0 |

### 2. Greeting & Professionalism — 15 pts
How the call was answered — the first impression a paying customer gets.

| Level | Points |
|---|---|
| Branded, warm greeting: business name + a name + offer to help ("Thanks for calling [Biz], this is Maria, how can I help?") | 15 |
| Business name stated, professional, but no name/offer to help | 10 |
| Generic "Hello?" / unclear if you reached the right place | 5 |
| Rushed, distracted, background noise, or unprofessional | 0–3 |

### 3. Lead Capture — 20 pts
Did they actually get information that lets them follow up with you?

| Level | Points |
|---|---|
| Captured name **and** phone/contact **and** the need | 20 |
| Captured the need + one contact detail | 12 |
| Talked through the need but took no contact info | 5 |
| Gave info/price and ended the call without capturing anything | 0 |

### 4. Conversion Action (Next Step) — 20 pts
Did they move you toward becoming a customer, or just answer and stop?

| Level | Points |
|---|---|
| Offered a concrete next step: booked/held an appointment, gave a quote + scheduled follow-up, or set a clear time to talk | 20 |
| Soft next step: "I'll have someone call you back" with a name or timeframe | 12 |
| Vague: "call us back if you want to schedule" (puts the work on you) | 6 |
| No next step offered at all | 0 |

### 5. After-Hours Coverage — 15 pts
What happens when someone calls outside open hours (or to an unmanned line). This is where most local businesses bleed the most leads.

| Level | Points |
|---|---|
| Live answer, answering service, or smart routing that can book/triage | 15 |
| Professional voicemail: states business name, hours, and a clear callback promise | 10 |
| Generic voicemail with no useful info | 5 |
| Rings forever, mailbox full, disconnected, or no after-hours path | 0 |

### 6. Follow-Up / Callback — 10 pts
You left a voicemail (or a missed call). Did they chase the lead?

| Level | Points |
|---|---|
| Called or texted back within ~1 business hour | 10 |
| Same day | 6 |
| Next day | 3 |
| Never (by 48h) | 0 |

**Total: /100.**

## Grade bands

Translate the number into a plain-English verdict on the scorecard.

| Score | Grade | Label (EN) | Label (ES) | What it means |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 90–100 | **A** | Locked Tight | Bien Cerrado | Rare. Leads rarely slip. Keep it up + monitor after-hours. |
| 75–89 | **B** | Solid — Minor Leaks | Sólido — Fugas Menores | Good core, one or two fixable gaps (usually after-hours or follow-up). |
| 60–74 | **C** | Leaking Leads | Perdiendo Clientes | Working hours OK, but real money slips through gaps. |
| 40–59 | **D** | Major Leaks | Fugas Importantes | Multiple breakdowns; a meaningful share of callers go elsewhere. |
| 0–39 | **F** | Critical | Crítico | Most prospects who call are lost. Highest-upside fix. |

Frame the result honestly but constructively. Even an F is good news *for them* — it means there's a large, recoverable pool of leads sitting on the table.

## Cost-of-the-Leak estimator (optional, owner-provided inputs)

A score is abstract; dollars aren't. If the owner shares a few numbers, the spreadsheet estimates the monthly revenue leaking out. **Label it clearly as an estimate** — it uses their inputs, not a claimed statistic, so it stays inside the style-guide rule on sourceable numbers.

```
Estimated missed/lost leads per month
  = inbound calls per week × 4.33 × (% not captured)

Estimated lost revenue per month
  = missed leads per month × close rate × average job/customer value
```

- **% not captured** is suggested by the score (e.g. an F implies a high leak rate); the owner can override with their own gut number.
- All three inputs (calls/week, close rate, avg value) come from the owner. If they don't know, leave it blank — never invent the figure.

---

## Delivering the scorecard

1. **Lead with the headline number + grade**, then the single biggest leak.
2. **Be specific and replayable:** "At 7:48pm I called and reached a voicemail that didn't say your hours or promise a callback — and no one called back by the next afternoon." Specifics prove you actually did the work.
3. **Give 2–3 fixes, ranked by impact.** Usually: after-hours coverage > callback speed > lead capture discipline.
4. **No hard pitch in the scorecard itself.** The honest evaluation *is* the value. The next conversation is where the solution comes up — and only if they want it.
5. **Bilingual:** the customer-facing summary ships in the owner's language (EN or ES). The spreadsheet's summary block carries both label sets.

---

## Consistency safeguards

- Always run Call #1 during their **posted** open hours — scoring "voicemail during open hours" only counts if you're sure they were open.
- Use a normal caller ID; blocked/unknown numbers change behavior and bias the test.
- Score from notes taken *during* the call, not memory afterward.
- One leak = one category. Don't double-penalize the same failure across categories.
- Re-run the exact same six categories every time. The spreadsheet enforces the point values so two evaluators land on the same score.
