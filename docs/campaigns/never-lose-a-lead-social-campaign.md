# "Never Lose a Lead Again" — Social Campaign (Facebook · LinkedIn · X)

**Goal:** Invite local service-business owners to a free 15-minute lead evaluation. The deliverable they receive: a **live mystery test call** (we call your business line like a real customer would) **plus a written lead-leak scorecard** showing exactly where leads slip through.

**Frame:** Founder-led, brand-new company asking for honest feedback. This is a strength — it lowers pressure, builds trust, and gives a reason to reach out that isn't "buy something."

**Compliance note (read before posting):** Everything below is built for **organic social posts** on Facebook, LinkedIn, and X — pages, profiles, and relevant local/industry groups. This is *not* cold SMS. Do **not** repurpose this copy as unsolicited text messages to business owners — that's an A2P 10DLC / TCPA consent problem (ironically the same rules MyOrbisVoice helps customers stay inside). Social DMs are fine *after* someone engages; unsolicited cold DMs at scale can also trip platform spam filters, so lead with the public post and let people opt in.

## Strategy at a glance

| Decision | Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Audience awareness stage | **Pain Aware** (Schwartz) | Owners feel the sting of missed calls/slow follow-up but haven't named it as a fixable system. Lead with the problem, not the product. |
| Framework | **PAS** (Problem → Agitate → Solve) | Default for cold social per style guide. |
| Tone tier | **Balanced** | Friendly, confident, soft urgency. No hype, no fake scarcity. |
| Cialdini stack | **Reciprocity** (free eval) + **Unity** (owner-to-owner) + **Scarcity** (limited eval slots, real) + light **Social Proof** (founding businesses) | New brand can't lean on big-number social proof yet, so Unity + Reciprocity carry the trust. |
| The ask | One small yes: "Want me to run the test?" | Commitment + Consistency — the eval is the first easy yes, not the sale. |

**Stat discipline:** The optional stat-anchored variants below use a `[CITE]` placeholder. Per the style guide, **do not publish an orphan number** — attach a real source chip (Forbes / HBR / BIA-Kelsey / vertical body) or cut the stat. Concrete-scenario variants (no stats) are safe to post as-is.

**Brand mention:** Posts are written brand-light so the "honest, founder-led" frame reads as genuine. Swap in "MyOrbisVoice" where marked `[brand optional]` if you want name recognition; leave it out to keep it personal.

## Posting notes

- **Rotate, don't repeat.** Post FB-1 to your page and one or two relevant local groups; use FB-2 as a follow-up a few days later. Don't paste identical copy into many groups at once — that reads as spam to both members and Facebook.
- **Scarcity must be real.** "A handful this week" / "unas pocas" only works if you actually cap it. If you can do 20, say a number you'll honor — fake limits burn trust (and the style guide forbids it).
- **LinkedIn:** lead with LI-1 as a normal post; save LI-2 for when you have a real, citable stat.
- **X:** X-1 for reach, X-2 thread when you want to tell the fuller story. Pin the one that performs.
- **One CTA per post.** Comment a keyword *or* DM — don't stack asks.
