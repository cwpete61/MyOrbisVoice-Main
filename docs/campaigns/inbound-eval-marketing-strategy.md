## 1. The Big Idea + 3 Best Taglines

**Big Idea: "The Phantom Customer" / "El Cliente Fantasma."**

The real prospect who already called, already wanted to buy, and silently left through a gap — no answer, late callback, dead after-hours line, English-only voicemail. They are invisible (they never show up in any report) but they cost real money, and the leak is fixable. The free Lead Capture Evaluation is the X-ray that makes the invisible visible: we place a real test call, then hand the owner a scorecard showing exactly where paying customers slip out.

This is the one owned idea every channel inherits — not 30 disconnected posts. It is honest by construction (a phantom customer is an inference from the owner's own gap, never an invented person or stat) and it gives every asset a job: social *names* the phantom, the eval *finds* them, the report *makes them visible*.

**Top 3 taglines (EN / ES):**

1. **"See where your customers slip out — before your competitor does."** / **"Descubre por dónde se te escapan los clientes — antes que tu competencia."** *(hero / primary)*
2. **"Meet the customers you never knew you lost."** / **"Conoce a los clientes que nunca supiste que perdiste."** *(curiosity / social header)*
3. **"Find your leaks in 15 minutes. Keep the report forever."** / **"Encuentra tus fugas en 15 minutos. Quédate el reporte para siempre."** *(offer / reciprocity)*

**North-star sentence (quote verbatim on site hero + partner pitch):**
EN: "We place one real test call to your business, like a customer would — then hand you a free scorecard showing exactly where a paying customer would slip away. No pitch. You keep it either way."
ES: "Hacemos una llamada de prueba real a tu negocio, como lo haría un cliente — y te entregamos un reporte gratis que muestra exactamente por dónde se te escaparía un cliente que paga. Sin venta. El reporte es tuyo de todas formas."

## 2. The Offer + Beta Framing + Opt-In Mechanic

**The offer.** A free 15-minute **Lead Capture Evaluation**. We place a real test call (plus an after-hours check) to the owner's business line and score six categories out of 100, returning a branded one-page scorecard: a letter grade (A–F), the single biggest leak, the 2–3 highest-impact fixes, and — optionally — a dollar estimate built from the owner's own numbers. **The eval IS the deliverable.** The owner keeps it whether or not we ever talk again.

**The six scored categories (the deliverable's backbone):**

| Category | Points |
|---|---|
| Speed to Answer | 20 |
| Greeting & Professionalism | 15 |
| Lead Capture | 20 |
| Conversion / Next Step Offered | 20 |
| After-Hours Coverage | 15 |
| Follow-Up / Callback | 10 |

**Beta framing — "new company" is the trust mechanism, not the apology.**
"Here's the honest truth: we're a brand-new company. We could buy ads and shout claims — instead we're earning trust the slow way, giving local owners a genuinely useful evaluation for free and letting the work speak. You keep the scorecard whether we ever talk again or not. That's the whole offer."
ES: "La verdad honesta: somos una empresa nueva… El reporte es tuyo, hablemos de nuevo o no. Esa es toda la oferta."

This converts the only real liability (being unknown) into the reason to say yes — Cialdini **Reciprocity** + **Unity** (owner-to-owner) carry the trust because we have no big-number social proof yet.

**The opt-in mechanic (one small yes per post):**
- **Comment a keyword** under the post → triggers a DM. Or **DM directly.**
- **Founding cohort cap:** a real, honored number — **"Founding 25"** per month, with a live counter ("9 of 25 open"). Scarcity is used **only because it's truly capped** — never a fake countdown, never a fake reopen.
- **Signature reciprocity line on every surface (button, post, PDF footer):** "You keep the scorecard either way." / "El reporte es tuyo de todas formas."

## 2b. Campaign angles to A/B test (added 2026-06-16)

Five distinct angles into the **same funnel** — test which pulls the most opt-ins. The Graphics generator carries each as a selectable track so partners make matching art.

| Track | What it tests | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Beta recruitment** | Honest "we need testers" ask. Disarms, justifies the test call, turns "new company" into the reason to say yes. | **Lead angle for cold** — the hard step is getting permission to call; "beta tester" justifies it best. |
| **Phantom Customer (pain)** | Emotional loss — the customer you never knew you lost. | Reinforcement + retargeting after the beta ask runs. |
| **Competitor steal** | Loss-aversion — the job went to whoever picked up first. | Sharper, slightly more aggressive. |
| **Honest math** | Anti-hype — no fake stats, your own numbers. | Differentiator + trust builder; converts skeptics. |
| **After-hours self-test** | Low-threat challenge — call your own line tonight. | Top-of-funnel engagement. |

**Recommended structure:** beta-recruitment is the cold front door (it flips the frame — they're helping *us*, guard drops, "what's the catch" dies); Phantom Customer is the emotional reinforcement in the body and retarget. Beta = *why it's free*; Phantom = *what's at stake*. Run all five as A/B, measure opt-in (comment/DM) rate per track, double down on the winner.

**Lead beta post (cold front door):**
EN: "Honest ask: MyOrbisVoice is brand new. We built a system that catches the customer calls businesses lose — missed calls, after-hours, no follow-up — and we need real local businesses to test it on. The deal: let us place a few test calls to your line, like a customer would. We score what happens — does the call turn into a booked sale, or quietly slip away? You get a free report with exactly what we found and how to fix it. Yours to keep whether we ever work together or not. Taking 25 businesses this round. Comment BETA."
ES: "Petición honesta: MyOrbisVoice es completamente nueva… Tomamos 25 negocios esta ronda. Escribe BETA."

## 3. Best Polls, Quiz, Post Series, Video/Carousel/DM

### Best Polls (Top 4)

Each maps 1:1 to a scored category. Vote = the easy first yes; pinned first comment invites the eval. Keyword **TEST / PRUEBA**.

**Poll 1 — The 7pm Saturday Call (Speed to Answer + After-Hours).** *FB + LinkedIn.*
EN: "A customer calls your business at 7pm on a Saturday. What actually happens?" — • A real person answers • Goes to voicemail • Rings out / not sure • I never thought about it
ES: "Un cliente llama a tu negocio un sábado a las 7pm. ¿Qué pasa de verdad?" — • Contesta una persona real • Cae en el buzón • Suena y nadie contesta / no estoy seguro • Nunca lo había pensado
*Lowest-threat opener; the "not sure"/"never thought about it" options self-identify the exact owner the eval converts.*

**Poll 2 — Out of 10 calls, how many become customers? (Conversion).** *LinkedIn primary.*
EN: • 7+ • 4–6 • 1–3 • Honestly, no idea / ES: • 7 o más • 4–6 • 1–3 • La verdad, ni idea
*Benchmark-style self-rating over-indexes on LinkedIn; "no idea" qualifies owners who lack measurement. This poll surfaces the cost-of-leak estimator in its CTA.*

**Poll 3 — How fast do you call a missed lead back? (Follow-Up).** *FB + LinkedIn.*
EN: • Within the hour • Same day • Next day • When I get to it / never / ES: • En menos de una hora • El mismo día • Al día siguiente • Cuando puedo / nunca
*The options ARE the scorecard's scoring bands — voters self-score and are primed to want the real number.*

**Poll 4 — What costs you more leads? (Diagnostic / closer).** *FB + LinkedIn.*
EN: "If your business loses leads, where's the leak?" — • Calls missed during the day • Slow / no callbacks • After-hours + weekends • Genuinely not sure
ES: • Llamadas perdidas durante el día • Devoluciones lentas o nulas • Fuera de horario + fines de semana • De verdad no estoy seguro
*The 4 options are the top eval categories — whichever an owner picks tells you their suspected leak before the call. Best used to CLOSE a poll rotation: it invites the eval as the resolution to all earlier polls.*

### Best Quiz (1, fully specified)

**"How leaky is your phone?" / "¿Qué tan fugado está tu teléfono?"** — Tier-2 / Balanced, soft labels, owner-input estimate only.

A 6-question self-scored quiz (one per eval category) hosted on the marketing site EN + ES, ending in a result tier + an **optional** cost-of-leak estimate built from the owner's own three numbers, explicitly labeled an estimate.

**Questions (single-select, each scored to its category's point weight):**

1. **Speed to Answer (20):** When a brand-new customer calls during business hours, they usually… (a) reach a live person in seconds [20] (b) wait through several rings [12] (c) get voicemail [4] (d) not sure [0]
2. **Greeting (15):** A stranger calling hears, in the first 5 seconds… (a) a name + warm "how can I help?" [15] (b) just the business name [9] (c) a quick "Hello?" [5] (d) a recording / no idea [0]
3. **Lead Capture (20):** When a new caller hangs up without booking, do you have their name + number? (a) always [20] (b) usually [13] (c) only if they booked [5] (d) often not [0]
4. **Conversion / Next Step (20):** At the end of a new-customer call, someone… (a) offers a clear next step every time [20] (b) usually [13] (c) only if asked [6] (d) rarely [0]
5. **After-Hours (15):** A customer calling after close gets… (a) a 24/7 answer or clean callback path [15] (b) a clear voicemail with hours [9] (c) a full/old mailbox [3] (d) no idea [0]
6. **Follow-Up (10):** You miss a call from a potential customer. You call back… (a) within the hour [10] (b) same day [6] (c) next day [3] (d) when I get to it / never [0]

**Result tiers (out of 100):**

| Score | Tier (EN / ES) | Message |
|---|---|---|
| 85–100 | **Sealed Tight / Bien Sellado** | "Few phantom customers escape you. A real test call confirms it — want proof on paper?" |
| 65–84 | **Minor Leaks / Fugas Menores** | "A few customers are slipping out. The free eval pinpoints exactly which gap and how to close it." |
| 40–64 | **Leaking / Con Fugas** | "Paying customers are getting away. The scorecard ranks your top 2–3 fixes by impact." |
| 0–39 | **Wide Open / Abierto de Par en Par** | "Good news — this is the most recoverable kind of problem. Let's run a real test call and map it." |

**Result-page CTA + estimate:** "Want the real version? We place an actual test call and hand you the branded scorecard — free, yours to keep. Optionally, give us 3 numbers (calls/week, close rate, avg job value) and we'll add a **labeled estimate** of monthly revenue leaking — your math, not a made-up stat." → opt-in to Founding 25.

## 5. The Funnel: Social → Opt-In → Free Eval → Report → Conversation

1. **Social (Pain-Aware).** Poll/post/video names the Phantom Customer and the specific leak. One small yes: comment a keyword or DM. *Cialdini: Unity + Reciprocity; no hard sell.*
2. **Opt-in (micro-commitment).** Keyword triggers DM sequence; owner gives best number + 15-min window. Founding-25 cap creates honest urgency. Call-recording consent captured here.
3. **Free Eval (the deliverable).** We place a real test call + after-hours check, score the 6 categories, observe the actual gaps. The call IS the test — nothing faked.
4. **Branded Report (reciprocity made concrete).** One-page scorecard: grade, biggest leak, top 2–3 fixes ranked by impact, optional owner-input dollar estimate. Footer: "Yours to keep. No subscription, no obligation." **No hard pitch inside the report.**
5. **Conversation (only if invited).** "If you'd ever like help closing these gaps, we're one message away — only if you want it." The owner can fix most issues themselves from the page; that self-sufficiency paradoxically raises trust and conversion. Soft transition to the product happens here, on the owner's timeline.

**Leading indicators to watch:** poll votes → keyword comments/DMs → opt-ins → evals delivered → reports → reply-to-talk rate. The opt-in count is the campaign's north-star leading metric.

## 6. Honesty + Trust Guardrails

- **No fabricated stats — ever.** Any "lost revenue" figure is an **estimate** built live from the owner's own three numbers (calls/week × close rate × avg job value × measured not-captured %) and **explicitly labeled an estimate.** Never cite a statistic we can't source.
- **The "$4,800" anti-pattern is banned.** We explicitly reject scary internet numbers in copy (Post 5) — the honesty is the differentiator.
- **The test call is real.** We actually place it; observations are facts ("at 7:48pm I hit a voicemail that didn't state your hours"), not claims.
- **Scarcity must be enforceable.** "Founding 25" is a real, honored cap with a live counter. No fake countdowns, no fake reopens. Only show "limited slots" when truly capped.
- **Call-recording consent.** If a call is recorded, we tell the owner and they can decline — captured in the DM sequence before the eval.
- **No hard pitch in the report.** The scorecard is self-sufficient value; the owner keeps it regardless. Soft, invited conversation only.
- **Self-sourced sample data only.** "We called 10 [vertical]s" uses the founder's own real calls; sample scorecards are clearly labeled fictional/hypothetical.
- **Beta status stated plainly.** "New company" is disclosed, not hidden — it's the trust mechanism (Reciprocity + Unity), not a liability to mask.
- **Bilingual parity.** EN + ES ship together on every post, poll, quiz, video caption, carousel, and DM. No English-only "for now." ES uses LatAm informal **tú**; legal/estimate disclaimers translated, not dropped.
- **Aggression tier: Balanced** (default for home services/beauty/fitness); **Conservative-safe variants** for dental & legal (Posts 2 and 6 are already written to that bar — no hype, logic-led).
