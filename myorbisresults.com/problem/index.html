The Real Problem

You don't have one problem. You have a bucket with six holes.

A new tool plugs one hole. The other five keep draining. That's why "we tried marketing and it didn't work" is so common — the leak was never the marketing. It was the whole bucket. Here's each hole, and what it quietly costs you.

Dead Money

Every lead you ever collected and never followed up with is money already spent that produced nothing. They didn't say no — they just never heard from you again.

Cost: revenue you already paid to acquire, sitting idle.

Invisible Online

If you're not in the Google Map Pack top three for "[your service] near me," buyers never see you. Thin reviews and a neglected profile keep you off the page where the decision happens.

Cost: the customers who chose a competitor they could find.

Cold Leads

A ready-to-buy caller hits voicemail or waits hours for a callback. By then they've called the next name on the list and booked. Speed-to-lead isn't a nicety — it's who wins the job.

Cost: hot demand handed to whoever answered first.

No Sales Process

Calls get winged. One staffer books everyone, another books no one, and nobody knows why. Without a repeatable process and trained handoffs, close rates stay low and inconsistent.

Cost: deals lost on the phone you already paid to ring.

Wasted Marketing

You pay for ads and referrals, the leads come in, and then… nothing. No nurture, no follow-up sequence, no second touch. The spend converts a fraction of what it should because the back half is missing.

Cost: most of your ad budget, quietly.

Owner Drowning

You're answering phones, chasing quotes, and posting reviews at 11pm instead of running the business. The busywork crowds out the two things that actually grow revenue: selling and serving.

Cost: your time, and the growth it could have bought.
Why patching fails

Fix one hole, the bucket still drains.

A chatbot doesn't fix invisibility. SEO doesn't answer the phone. A new website doesn't reactivate dead leads. Each point solution ignores the other five holes — so the water level never rises and you conclude "marketing doesn't work for us." The problem was never the tool. It's that the holes are connected, and only a connected system seals all of them at once.

How To Spot Yours

Five quick checks that tell you where you're leaking.

You don't need an audit to feel the leaks — you can see most of them in an afternoon. Search "[your service] near me" on Google: if you're not in the top three of the Map Pack, that's Invisible Online. Check your Google Business Profile review count against the competitor above you. Look at your missed-call log and ask how many got a callback within five minutes — slow speed-to-lead is Cold Leads. Pull the last 50 leads from your inbox and count how many got a second touch; the rest are Dead Money. And ask yourself who's answering the phone at 7pm — if it's voicemail, or you, that's two more holes.

Each check maps to one of the six leaks above — and to the part of the MyOrbisResults system built to seal it.
