✗ Scene one. Tuesday at 1:48 PM. Without your new team member.
Maria calls. The phone rings. Voicemail picks up.

A regular customer calls. Her name is Maria. She's been coming to your salon for three years. You know her. You like her. She brought her sister last month. She has a daughter named Sofía. Her birthday is in two weeks.

She calls. The phone rings. You're with another customer in the chair. Voicemail picks up. She hangs up because she doesn't want to leave a message. She'll call back later, she thinks. She doesn't. Two weeks later, her birthday comes and goes and you didn't reach out. She drifts.

✓ Scene one. Tuesday at 1:48 PM. With your new team member.
Maria calls. The agent picks up. Maria forwards the birthday text to her sister.

She calls. The agent picks up. "Hi Maria, good to hear from you. Are you looking to book your usual?" (The CRM knows her usual.) Maria books for Friday at 3 PM. The agent texts a confirmation and adds a note about Sofía starting kindergarten next month.

On her birthday, an automated text goes out with $20 off and asks how Sofía's first week of school went. Maria forwards the text to her sister with a heart emoji.

That's one customer. One Tuesday. One $200 booking saved. One referral. One relationship deepened.

✗ Scene two. Friday at 7:47 PM. Without your new team member.
A new prospect calls. Voicemail picks up. He books with your competitor before bed.

He calls. Voicemail picks up (you closed at 5). He hangs up. He won't call back tomorrow. He'll Google the next business in your category before he goes to bed. They'll pick up. He'll book with them.
